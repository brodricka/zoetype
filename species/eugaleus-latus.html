Eugaleus latus

Eugaleus latus is known entirely from its teeth. No skeleton, no soft tissue, no preserved behavior. Just the shape of what it bit and how those edges wore down over time. You are the same: the record of you is written in what you have pressed against, what you have held, what you have ground down through years of quiet, deliberate contact.

Houndsharks hunt through patience, using the cold geometry of positioning rather than raw speed. They wait for the angle to close on its own. You work the same way: you read the structure of a situation before you enter it, and when you move, you move because the moment is already decided. You do not rush conclusions. You let them arrive through accumulated pressure, the way a tooth wears a groove not in one strike but across a thousand small ones. When others are still scanning for the problem, you have already identified the leverage point and are applying weight to it.

A predator that shapes itself to the contours of an environment does not just feed, it organizes the behavior of everything around it. Prey species route differently because it exists. The whole system recalibrates around its presence. When you disengage, the people around you lose the thing that was quietly structuring their choices, the steadying pressure they did not know they were leaning on. Your absence creates a specific kind of drift. You are the fixed point that others triangulate from without knowing they are doing it.

Shadow
Ambush patience, taken far enough, becomes a refusal to arrive. Eugaleus latus held its position until the conditions were right, but for you that same calculation can stretch into indefinite stillness, a siege that costs you the thing you were waiting to take. You calculate exits before you have fully entered rooms. The ruthlessness you apply to situations you also apply to people, and the cold persistence that makes you formidable reads, to the people close to you, as withholding. What it costs you specifically is intimacy: the people who want to reach you meet only positioning.
Species Fact
Eugaleus latus is an extinct species known only from fossilized teeth, meaning everything about its living behavior is inferred entirely from the shape and wear patterns of its ancient dental remains.

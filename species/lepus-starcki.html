Lepus starcki
Lepus starcki

Starck's hare lives above 3000 meters in the Afroalpine moorlands of Ethiopia, a landscape so extreme that almost no other mammal has figured out how to stay. You did not end up in the hard environment by accident. You are built for it, and the difficulty is precisely what cleared the competition.

This hare does not hunt in packs or navigate by consensus. It reads the terrain alone, moves alone, and when the moment demands speed it commits completely without waiting for confirmation from anyone nearby. You process the same way. You take in a situation, form your read, and act on it. Other people experience this as self-sufficiency. You experience it as the only sensible approach. The specific thing this produces in you is a very high tolerance for being the only one who sees what you see.

Starck's hare grazes the moorland grasses and sedges that define Afroalpine ground cover, and without consistent herbivore pressure those plant communities shift and compact in ways that alter everything above and below them. The systems you move through work better with you in them, even when your presence is quiet. When you withdraw, things drift. The specific fact is that you are one of the few in your environment who can function at the altitude everyone else finds unsustainable.

Shadow
When a predator appears, Starck's hare runs. It does not look back. The hare beside it is on its own. You carry this same wiring, and in moments of genuine threat you accelerate away from proximity without deliberation, leaving people who believed they were close to you suddenly standing in open ground. This is explosive, efficient, and it works for survival. What it costs is the specific trust that only builds between people who have stayed in a hard moment together, and that trust, once broken by flight, rarely reassembles at the same elevation.
Species Fact
Lepus starcki is endemic to the high-altitude Afroalpine moorlands of Ethiopia, surviving in one of Africa's most extreme montane environments above 3000 meters where few other mammals can thrive.

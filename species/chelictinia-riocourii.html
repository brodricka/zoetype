Chelictinia riocourii
Chelictinia riocourii

Your tail is nearly as long as your body, and it moves like scissors cutting air. The African Swallow-tailed Kite carries this forked tail as its primary instrument of flight, using subtle adjustments of those two long feathers to redirect its body mid-pursuit without a single extra wingbeat. You operate the same way. The precision others mistake for effortlessness is actually a highly tuned physical system, one you have refined so thoroughly that the work has become invisible.

This bird hunts on the wing over arid savanna, catching insects in flight, reading thermals, holding altitude across hours of open sky. It does not wait at a perch. It stays in motion and processes the environment in real time, adjusting trajectory before most birds have registered that something moved. You think best when moving through a problem rather than sitting with it. You gather information the way the kite gathers prey, by staying in the current of what is actually happening, not by withdrawing to analyze from a distance. The result is that your read on a situation is often faster and more accurate than people expect, because you were already tracking it before they thought to ask.

Across the Sahel and arid West Africa, this kite controls insect populations that would otherwise spike and strip vegetation bare. Remove it, and the balance tips. The systems you move through depend on your presence in ways that only become obvious when you step back. You regulate things by being in motion through them, and your disengagement reads as withdrawal even when it is temporary. The people around you lose a kind of aerial surveillance they did not know they were relying on.

Shadow
The scissor-tailed kite does not share airspace gracefully when resources thin. It outcompetes smaller birds with the same aerial efficiency it uses to hunt, turning its greatest strength into a territorial weapon. When you feel the pressure of scarcity, whether material, social, or creative, you can become cutting in ways that track as precision but land as dominance. You move so fast through conflict that slower-processing people never get footing before the outcome is decided. That speed wins the exchange and damages the relationship, and you often do not notice the damage until long after the thermals have shifted.
Species Fact
Chelictinia riocourii, the African Swallow-tailed Kite, has an extraordinarily forked tail resembling scissors that can span nearly as long as its body, giving it extraordinary aerial maneuverability that allows it to catch insects on the wing while barely flapping.
